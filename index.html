Plastic Ocean

Tan Zi Xi, the Singaporean artist recognized as MessyMsxi, ingeniously crafts collections that playfully yet poignantly unveil the stark truths of ocean pollution. In a remarkable endeavor, Tan Zi Xi meticulously gathered, purified, and systematically arranged 500 kilograms of abandoned ocean plastic, totaling over 26,000 individual pieces, to bring to life her captivating installation titled "Plastic Ocean." This immersive exhibition, featuring suspended plastic debris in a mesmerizing display, serves as a visual narrative, effectively highlighting the profound impact of plastic waste on our global marine ecosystems, illustrating how they are slowly suffocated by this pervasive environmental challenge.

Khian Sea

Renowned for his work in collages and photography, Benjamin West has undertaken numerous projects addressing environmental concerns. Central to Benjamin's artistic narrative is our intricate relationship with nature, seamlessly weaving from the organic to the technological in his cartographies. Through layering of organic elements upon mechanical and artificial visuals, Benjamin crafts Khain Sea - aesthetically stunning, surreal - all while carrying subtle political undertones. Through these deliberate juxtapositions, Benjamin prompts contemplation on the extent of our detachment from Mother Earth and challenges us to confront the adverse impact we have imposed on the planet.

Birds Watching

Jenny Kendler is the creative mind behind this sculpture, Birds Watching, a striking 40-foot installation featuring a 'flock' of one hundred reflective bird's eyes meticulously mounted on aluminum. Each eye in this immersive artwork represents a bird species threatened by climate change in the United States. The sculpture's commanding presence, accentuated by the gaze of these eyes, appears to lay bare the observers. Unlike our typical one-sided engagement with media, Jenny's avian eyes demand active participation from onlookers in the act of observation. The exhibition posits that we cannot absolve ourselves of responsibility in the face of the climate crisis and emphasizes our obligation to take meaningful action.

Pile

Gavin turk presents bag full of discarded products, unrecycled organic matter thrown in with the by-products of our wasteful consumerist lifestyles. Here, we are forced to consider the consequences of a consumerist lifestyle, that only results in a larger pile growing not only on land, but in the oceans too. Turk comments are defined by what we throw away and conversely we are deconstructed by what we choose to display in our hallowed museum halls.

Ice Watch

Ice Watch, by Olafur Eliasson, is a perfect example of how the idea of experiencing the reality of climate change. Visitors who encountered the 120 tonnes of Greenlandic glacial ice in London were able to reach out and touch the effects of a changing environment. Olafur hopes that this experience will motivate people to become involved and act together to pressure our governments and businesses to make changes on a systemic level, which is the only way we will succeed in responding to this crisis.
